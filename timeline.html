Timeline

HOW IT STARTED (After WWII)
· Truman discovered Americans in New Mexico made an atomic weapon
· Thought to be made useful
· Created a shift balance in power
· Aggression came and wanted to secure soviet territories
· Military divisions occurred in Berlin (West and East Germany)
· George Kennan sends long telegram
· Respect for Russian people but negative views towards communist leadership began
· After Roosevelt's death, he sent a long telegram
· Caused sensation in Washington and aggression from Stalin
· Led to Truman relying on military and economic forces when dealing with the soviets
THE IRON CURTAIN (march 5, 1946)
· Political boundary describing the division in Europe
· The Soviet Union wanted to seal off itself and its allies from open contact and noncommunist
· Stalin never risked major war but wanted to undermine major western powers
· March 12, 1947, Truman supplied Greece and Turkey against the Soviet powers
LOYALTY PROGRAM (March 2, 1948)
· Check the loyalty of federal employees (avoid cold war spies)
· Berlin Blockade begins (June 24, 1948)
· Soviet Union blocked rail, road, and water access to allied parts
· US responded with airlift
THE MARSHALL PLAN (April 3, 1948)
· US program that helped recover western and southern European countries to have stable living conditions after WWII
· Aid was offered to all countries but the Soviets withdrew from the plan
· Very successful, Truman extended program to more countries
VIETNAM WAR (November 1, 1955- April 30, 1975)
· North Vietnam (ran by communist party) fought south Vietnam because of ties with communism and was supported by the US
CUBAN MISSILE CRISIS (October 16- October 28, 1962)
· Installation of nuclear-armed Soviets
· JFK notified Americans and caused tension
· Solved in discreet meeting (taking down nuclear site exchange for not invading Cuba)
END OF COLD WAR (December 26, 1991)
· The soviet union itself caused its own downfall
· Every communist government replaced theirs with a noncommunist government
· Berlin Wall was destroyed
